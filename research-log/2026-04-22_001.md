## 1. Objective
Evaluate the structural stability and flexibility of lanmodulin under acidic conditions (progressive protonation from pH 6 to pH 2.5), and identify residues potentially responsible for instability or altered dynamics.

---

## 2. Background / Rationale
Lanmodulin is being engineered to function under mining-relevant acidic conditions (target ~pH 2.5). Protonation at low pH is expected to disrupt charge-dependent interactions, particularly in lanthanide-binding sites and flexible regions.
An extremophilic reference organism, Methylacidiphilum fumariolicum, was identified as a potential model due to its ability to utilize lanthanides under acidic and high-temperature conditions. Comparative analysis is planned.

---

## 3. Inputs

**Protein / Structure:**

* Protein: Lanmodulin 
* PDB ID: 6MI5
* Variant: Wild Type (WT)

**Conditions:**

* pH values tested: 6 → 2.5 (stepwise)
* Temperature: default (not explicitly modeled)

**Tools Used:**

* ChimeraX (structure visualization)
* H++ (protonation states)
* CABSflex 2.0 (flexibility simulation)
* Google Colab (Python preprocessing)

---

## 4. Method
1. Retrieved lanmodulin PDB structure from RCSB Protein Data Bank.
2. Visualized structure using ChimeraX.
3. Submitted structure to H++ for protonation at selected pH values (starting at pH 6, decreasing to pH 2.5).
4. Processed H++ output files using a custom Python script (Google Colab) to remove incompatible formatting and extraneous data.
5. Submitted cleaned structures to CABSflex 2.0 for flexibility simulation.
6. Extracted RMSF profiles for residue-level fluctuation analysis.

* Protonation at low pH likely alters electrostatic interactions, contributing to increased flexibility in both binding sites and distal structural regions.
* Residues within binding sites are critical but may be less suitable for direct mutation due to functional constraints.
* Loop and hinge regions emerge as initial candidates for modification due to their potential role in global stability without directly disrupting metal coordination.
